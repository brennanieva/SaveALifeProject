The Inner Layer

The Retina: Makes up the inner layer of the back of the eyeball

The Retina Inner Neural Layer Contains

  • Neurons
  • Photoreceptors
  • Ganglion Neurons
  • Forms optic nerve
  • Bipolar Neurons

Rods
Register a grayscale of black and white
Rule your peripheral vision
(side vision; what is seen on the side by the eye when looking straight ahead.)

Cones
Detect fine detail and color and can be divided into

  • Red-sensitive types
  • Green-sensitive types
  • Blue-sensitive types
